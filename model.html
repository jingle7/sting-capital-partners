The Model

Brute force, then restraint.

Most capital introduction runs on relationships and guesswork. Ours runs on exhaustive validation, and on the discipline to throw most of the results away.

Exhaustively selective90%+ of pairings rejected
Individualized outputNo two funds, one list
Strictly confidentialBy inquiry only; never shared

Every pairing, tested.

The model evaluates every possible pairing between your fund and the institutional investor universe: pensions, endowments, foundations, funds of funds, OCIOs, sovereign and family capital, and the dedicated programs many of them run.

Each pairing is scored against your target asset class, your strategy and stage, and the specific requirements of each investor's mandate or program. Nothing is assumed from a name on a list.

The discipline

90%+

Compatibility is not enough. The model discards more than ninety percent of all candidates, keeping only the matches that clear a high bar on mandate fit, program eligibility, and asset-class alignment. A shorter list is the point.

Built for your fund alone.

No two funds receive the same list. The output is built around your strategy, your stage, and the capital you're raising. Every entry earns its place by fitting your raise specifically.

Every match arrives with the rationale for why it qualifies, so your team can move straight to outreach instead of re-doing the research.

The model itself is built, maintained, and run entirely in-house by the firm's founding partners, James Ingle (MIT) and Owen Strayer (Boston College). Nothing about your raise is outsourced.
